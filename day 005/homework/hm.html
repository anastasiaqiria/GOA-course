About Sherlock Holmes

Sherlock Holmes is one of the most famous detectives in literature, a mind like a locked room full of ticking clocks. Created by Arthur Conan Doyle, Holmes first appeared in 1887 and has been solving impossible crimes ever since. Holmes lives at 221B Baker Street in London and is known for razor-sharp observation and logical deduction. He notices the details others stroll past, a smudge of mud, a frayed cuff, a nervous pause, then builds the truth step by step. His loyal friend and narrator, Dr. John Watson, records these cases, giving readers a front-row seat to Holmes thinking. The stories span novels and short tales collected in the Sherlock Holmes. Famous adventures include A Study in Scarlet, The Hound of the Baskervilles, and The Sign of the Four. Together, they mix mystery, clever twists, and the foggy atmosphere of Victorian England. Holmes influence is enormous. He helped define the modern detective genre and inspired countless adaptations in books, films, and TV shows. More than a century later, his methods still feel fresh, proof that a keen eye and a calm mind never go out of style
