ABOUT WINTER OLYMPICS

The Winter Olympic Games are a major international sports event where athletes compete in sports played on snow and ice. They are organized by the International Olympic Committee and take place every four years. The first Winter Olympics were held in 1924 in Chamonix. Athletes from many countries compete in different winter sports such as skiing, snowboarding, ice hockey, figure skating, and bobsleigh. These sports require great skill, balance, and speed. The Winter Olympics bring together athletes from all around the world and promote friendship, fair competition, and peace between nations. Today the Games include more than 100 events in many winter sports disciplines.

GEORGIA AND WINTER OLYMPICS

Georgia has been participating in the Winter Olympics as an independent country since 1994. Georgian athletes compete in sports such as alpine skiing and figure skating. At the 2026 Winter Olympics in Italy, Georgia was represented by eight athletes. They competed in alpine skiing and figure skating. A historic moment for Georgia happened in 2026 when figure skaters Anastasiia Metelkina and Luka Berulava won the countrys first ever Winter Olympic medal, earning a silver medal in pair figure skating. This achievement was very important for Georgian sports. Georgia continues to develop winter sports, and young athletes hope to win more medals in future Winter Olympic Games.
